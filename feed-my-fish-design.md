# Feed My Fish — Design Notes

## One-sentence pitch
A gentle alarm-clock game where you wake up and care for a small living world before starting the day.

## Core loop (version 1)

Morning sequence:
1. Alarm rings
2. Character wakes in simple room
3. Slide door open
4. Walk to garden
5. Feed koi
6. Fish gather, water ripples
7. Soft prompt: "What intention do you want to set today?"

Reward: fish appear, garden evolves, soundscape deepens.

## What makes it special

**Sound design** (big)
- Bamboo stretch
- Bonsai clipping
- Ink brush swish
- Fire crackle
- Water running
Calm apps are 90% sound design.

**Ink / paper aesthetic**
- Ink pen linework
- Paper background texture
- Simple, lightweight shapes

**Time-of-day world**
- Morning: light tan paper, warm light
- Night: deep indigo sky, stars, lanterns
- The world feels alive because it mirrors the real clock

## Behavioral loop
care → movement → reflection → motivation

Not about productivity. Not about streaks or metrics.
About tending a small world. That distinction matters.

> "Sometimes a pet is all that keeps us getting out of bed."

Psychologically: gives people a low-stakes reason to begin the day.

## Version 0.1 scope
Build only:
- Room
- Door
- Garden
- Pond
- Fish
- Feed action
- Intention prompt
- Day/night color shift

Everything else is a future expansion.

## Future expansions
- Cats, birds, small animals visiting the garden
- Bamboo that grows over days
- Bonsai you trim occasionally
- Seasonal weather (rain, snow, cherry blossoms)
- Fire / lanterns at night
- Ambient sounds for each scene element
- Marauder's Map style reveal of the world as you return over time
- The world becomes richer the longer you tend it

## Reference touchstones
- Animal Crossing (daily ritual, small world evolving)
- Monument Valley (ink/paper aesthetic, quiet wonder)
- Calm / Headspace (intention setting, morning ritual)

Sits in the space between: gentle game, meditation app, daily habit tracker.

## Design principles
- Abundance mindset, not scarcity
- Familiarity built, not assumed
- Tending, not achieving
- Orient first, steps follow
